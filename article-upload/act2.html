Teaching & Learning Activity #3

Questions

Here are the questions of this activity:

  1. Implement RSA with modulus 55 and public exponent 3 by finding p, q, and d. Given a plaintext m with GCD(m,55)=1, prove that m = cd (mod 55), where c is me (mod 55).

  2. We can implement 2RSA by choosing a modulus n and two public exponents e1 and e2. In this case, encryption is done using: c1 = me1 (mod n) followed by c2 = c1e2 (mod n). Is 2RSA stronger than 1RSA? Juystify your answer.

  3. Why are the public exponents e=1 and e=2 never used in RSA?

  4. Two users on a network use RSA moduli n1 and n2 that are not equal and not relatively prime. Can you attack them?

  5. Given a large prime n you compute k = s(n-1)/2 (mod n) and find that k != +/-1. You compute x = k2 (mod n). Prove that if x != 1 then n is not prime, and that if x = 1 then we can factor n.

  6. You send your Visa card number to two e-commerce sites that use RSA with the same modulus but different public exponents e1 and e2 that are relatively prime. Show that by intercepting the two transmissions, Eve can find your credit card data.

  7. Show that RSA works even if GCD(m,n) != 1.

  8. In order to guard against the short message attack, Alice increases the size of m by either (1) appending 100 zeros to it or (2) repeating it two times (either paddings must be public accordingly to Kerckhoff's). Does either thwart the short message attack?
