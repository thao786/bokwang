How to Meditate

There're 2 kinds of meditations: Mantra and Zazen. All methods of meditation you hear is a variation of one those 2 forms.

Mantra

Mantra meditation is giving the mind something to do to discipline it. The most common variation is observing the breathe by counting to 10, or saying a word when inhale and saying another when exhale. It doesn't matter what the word is as the purpose of this exercise is to tie the mind down by forcing it to do one thing only.

How to do Mantra Meditation

You have total control of how you want to do mantra. As you inhale, say the word "soft" in your mind, and as you exhale, say the word "softer". If your breath is too short for both, you can just say "softer" when you exhale. It doesn't matter what the technique is as long as you are persistent with it.




Zazen

Zazen meditation is to observe the mind. The priciple is to observe everything presently unfolding in your thoughts and sensations. Your job is to be one step above your thought, observe it but not entangle with it. Let the thought move through you.

Zazen meditation could be exciting as the mind would throw the craziest imaginations or dig up long gone memories in order to pull you out of focus. Your job is to see it, and not touch it. Just sit back and watch the movie.

Structure your meditation

A beginner should include both of those meditation methods. A session would begin with 3 minutes of mantra meditations following by 20 minutes of Zazen (or more). One should do 2 sessions a day, before starting the day and before bed.
